About This Application

This application provides two validated cardiovascular disease (CVD) risk calculators used in clinical practice. These tools estimate an individual's probability of experiencing a major cardiovascular event — such as a heart attack or stroke — over the next 10 years.

Risk calculators are important clinical decision-support tools. They help guide conversations between patients and their healthcare providers about preventive strategies, including lifestyle modifications and medications such as statins.

Available Calculators

ASCVD Risk Calculator

The Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Disease (ASCVD) risk calculator uses the Pooled Cohort Equations (PCE), developed jointly by the American College of Cardiology (ACC) and the American Heart Association (AHA) in 2013.

This calculator estimates the 10-year risk of a first ASCVD event (non-fatal myocardial infarction, coronary heart disease death, or fatal or non-fatal stroke) for patients aged 40–79 years.

  • Validated for White and African American adults
  • Age range: 40–79 years
  • Inputs: age, sex, race, cholesterol levels, blood pressure, smoking status, diabetes
Open ASCVD Calculator

PREVENT Calculator

The Predicting Risk of Cardiovascular Disease EVENTs (PREVENT) equations were published by the American Heart Association in 2023 as an updated, race-free replacement for the Pooled Cohort Equations.

PREVENT improves on prior models by including kidney function (eGFR), covering a broader age range (30–79 years), and removing race as a variable, making it applicable to all adult patients.

  • Applies to adults regardless of race or ethnicity
  • Age range: 30–79 years
  • Inputs: age, sex, cholesterol, blood pressure, smoking, diabetes, kidney function (eGFR)
